Success

Hos successus alit, possunt quia posse videntur.

Virgile  Enéide V, 231

These guys, the success nourishes them: they can because it looks as if they can.

Optimism

Florebo quocumque ferar.

Compagnie française des indes orientales

I will bloom wherever I am carried.

Sleep

Tempus erat quo prima quies mortalibus aegris

Incipit et dono divum gratissima serpit.

Virgile  Enéide II, 268

It was the hour when the first sleep begins for the unfortunate mortals and, by a gift from the gods, slips into them as welcome.

Happiness

Felix qui potuit rerum cognoscere causas

Atque metus omnes et inexorabile fatum

Subjecit pedibus strepitumque Acherontis avari

Virgile  Géorgiques II, 490-493

Blessed is he who has been able to understand nature and trample on all his fears, the inexorable fate and the din of the Acheron who returns nothing.
